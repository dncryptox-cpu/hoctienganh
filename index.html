Unit 1
Meeting the Guide & Trail Briefing
Alex Hello everyone! Welcome to the mountain trail. My name is Alex, and I will be your guide today. Are you ready for the hike?
Sarah Hi Alex! I am Sarah. Yes, I am very excited, but a little nervous. Is the trail very difficult?
Alex Nice to meet you, Sarah. Do not worry — the first part is easy, but it gets steeper later. Do you have enough water and snacks in your backpack?
Sarah Yes, I have two bottles of water and some energy bars. Should I wear my jacket now?
Alex No, it is quite warm right now — keep it in your bag. We will take short breaks every thirty minutes. Let's start walking and enjoy the beautiful nature!
Unit 2
On the Trail — Conditions & Getting Help
Alex How is everyone doing? We are halfway to the top. Sarah, are your hiking boots comfortable?
Sarah Yes, they are fine, thank you. But my backpack feels a bit heavy. Can we stop for a moment? I need to catch my breath.
Alex Of course. Let's take a five-minute break here by the river. Remember to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
Sarah Look at that view! The mountains are stunning from here. How much longer until we reach the campsite?
Alex About two more hours. The path ahead is a bit rocky, so please watch your step. Keep a steady pace and we will be there before sunset.
Unit 3
Reaching the Campsite — Reflecting & Planning
Sarah We finally reached the campsite, and I feel amazing. The trek was challenging, especially the rocky sections — but the beautiful scenery made it worth it. I learned that good communication on the trail is very important. Alex was great at giving clear instructions and keeping everyone safe. I also realized that pacing myself is better than rushing. Now I am sitting by the campfire, drinking hot tea, and looking at the stars. This is a wonderful experience, and I cannot wait for tomorrow's hike!
